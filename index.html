DeMT

Device-Guided Music Transfer

paper|code|

Annonymous Authors

Abstract. Device-aware music synthesis can mimic playback quality across a variety of devices or emulate device-specific characteristics for users lacking access to specific hardware. Existing works largely overlook the incorporation of conditional device characteristics when mainly employing limited standard numerical representations. Additionally, we observe that the integration of recent large vision language models (VLMs) and frequency response curves (FRCs) holds significant potential in providing meaningful device features yet also remains unexplored. Therefore, we propose DeMT, a novel approach that combines textual prompts and FRCs as line graphs, processed by a well-pre-trained VLM to extract relevant semantic embeddings. These embeddings condition a hybrid transformer demuc model with feature-wise linear modulation layers, enabling efficient and interpretable device-specific transformations. Fine-tuned on a self-collected music dataset of off-the-shelf playback devices, our system supports few-shot and zero-shot adaptation to unseen devices and enables practical applications such as device-style augmentation, spectral fidelity adjustment, and personalized music-to-music transformation.
